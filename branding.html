Program branding to Inspire

Elements

ICON Eventmore_vert
ICON Eventclose

ICON is a yearly event that just celebrated it's 5th year. Each year, the program is branded with a different look and at times new things are added to the program. This year, we built our theme around the elements. In order to sustain a program lasting over six months, the decision was made to start with a flat design and at the final event in Miami, switch to realism. This allowed me to use two design styles on the same program, at the same time.

Poster Designmore_vert
Poster Designclose

Each year, posters are created to help announce the theme and are used during registration events. With it being the 5th year, we were requested to find a way to include the Roman Numeral for 5 in the poster. I was able to use photos from the previous 4 years to create a mosaic. The photos would grab the attention of people during registration events, which gave an opportunity for the promoters to strike up a conversation.

Secondary Logosmore_vert
Secondary Logosclose

The secondary logos were created to go along with the flat, traditional logo and could be used together, or by themselves. The logos were designed by starting with circles of the same size, and then shaped to represent each of the elements.

Product Brandingmore_vert
Product Brandingclose

Product branding was very important for the event, as several material items were sent out like invitations, trophies, and promotional items. The image depicts a box concept that was pitched to be used for the invitation. The box would have been made of wood and had the name of the participant etched into it. The top would have a vinyl overlay so when the box was closed, the elements would be behind each of the letters being represented in the logo cutout on the box sleeve.

Real Elementsmore_vert
Real Elementsclose

Brining the on site event to life required several items to be created, including a video to kick off the awards show. The idea was to have real people handling elements, all created in 3D to provide as much of a realistic look as possible.
